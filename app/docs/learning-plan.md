# Japanese Learning Plan

## Phases

### Prerequisite — Weeks 1-3
Hiragana and katakana, taught alongside example vocabulary so characters are
never memorized in isolation.

**Status in this app:** all 46 hiragana ship in `src/data/hiragana.ts` with
three colloquial example words each. Katakana set is pending (see `TODO.md`).

### Phase 1 — Weeks 4-5: Foundation
Build the spine of daily speech via:
- **Survival phrases** — greetings, requests, apologies, basic responses.
- **Particles** — は, が, を, に, で, へ, と, も, から, まで, の, か, ね, よ, etc.
- **Essential verbs** — する, ある, いる, 行く, 来る, 食べる, 飲む, 見る, 聞く,
  読む, 書く, 話す, 言う, 分かる, 思う, 知る (and their colloquial forms).
- **Common adjectives** — い-adjectives and な-adjectives in everyday use
  (おいしい, 大きい, 小さい, いい, 悪い, 楽しい, 静か, etc.).

### Phase 2 — Weeks 6-8: Patterns and Vocabulary
Sentence-pattern mastery plus ~200 words across practical categories:
food, family, time, places, transportation, body, weather, work, school,
emotions, numbers/counters.

### Phase 3 — Weeks 9+: Colloquial
The colloquial layer that textbooks underweight:
- Casual sentence endings (だ/だよ/だね/じゃん/だろ).
- Contractions (〜てる, 〜とく, 〜ちゃう, 〜じゃう).
- Filler words (えーと, あのー, まあ, やっぱり, とりあえず).
- Slang and modern expressions (やばい, めっちゃ, 全然 in its colloquial use,
  だるい, etc.).

## Philosophy
> Learn the ~100 words that appear in 80% of conversation, then expand.

Casual forms come before formal speech, because the user wants to *speak with
people*, not pass JLPT N5. Formal language is a layer added later.

## Realistic Milestones
- **Weeks 1-3:** Read all kana.
- **Months 1-2:** Survival conversation (introductions, ordering, getting
  around).
- **Months 4-6:** Functional conversation with ~500 active words.
- **Year 1-2:** Comfortable daily conversation with ~1000+ active words.

## Daily Practice Shape
- 15-30 minute sessions.
- Three slots per session: **review** (SRS due cards), **new material**
  (one new concept or word batch), **active practice** (recording, quiz,
  or speaking aloud).
- Optional weekend deep dives into media (anime, podcasts, YouTube).

## Anti-patterns to Avoid
- Isolated kanji memorization without context.
- Front-loading formal/polite forms (です/ます-heavy textbooks).
- Romaji-only study — kana must come first.
- Word-by-word translation instead of chunked phrases.
- Skipping speaking practice "until I'm ready."
- Apps without spaced repetition.
